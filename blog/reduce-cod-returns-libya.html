How to Reduce COD Return Rates in Libya: 7 Proven Tactics

MDM Express Team July 7, 2026 11 min read

A 30% return rate in a COD operation doesn't just mean 30% of orders fail — it means you paid for advertising, packaging, and delivery on those orders and got nothing back. At scale, that's the difference between a profitable business and an operation that bleeds money.

These 7 tactics are used by the highest-performing COD sellers in Libya to consistently push delivery rates above 75% — some above 82%.

The 7 Tactics

01
Confirm Every Order by Phone Before Dispatch
Call every customer within 2–4 hours of their order. Confirm the product, price, address, and expected delivery time. This filters out unreachable numbers, fake orders, and impulse buyers who've already changed their minds — before you spend money on dispatch.
+15–20% delivery rate improvement
02
Collect Two Phone Numbers at Checkout
Add a second phone number field to your order form. When the first number is unreachable, you have a fallback. This alone prevents unnecessary dispatch of orders that would otherwise return because the delivery agent couldn't reach anyone.
+5–8% delivery rate improvement
03
Deliver Within 1–3 Days
Every day between order and delivery is a chance for the customer to change their mind. COD markets run on impulse — delivery speed is the difference between the customer still wanting the product and returning it on sight. Never sit on orders for more than 48 hours before dispatch.
Critical for impulse-purchase products
04
Improve Address Accuracy at Order Collection
Vague addresses ("near the main mosque") are a major cause of failed first-attempt deliveries. During the confirmation call, ask customers to specify their building, floor, nearest landmark, and preferred delivery time. The more specific the address, the higher the first-attempt success rate.
Reduces failed first attempts by 30–40%
05
Reattempt Returns Before Writing Them Off
Don't treat a returned order as a lost sale. Call the customer, understand what happened, and schedule a second delivery attempt. 30–40% of first-attempt returns successfully convert on redelivery — especially when the reason was "not available" rather than "changed mind."
Recovers 30–40% of returns
06
Tighten Your Facebook Ad Targeting
Broad, unfocused traffic generates high volume but low intent. Target by interest, behavior, and — critically — geographic area. Orders from cities your delivery partner covers well convert at much higher rates than orders from areas with slow or unreliable delivery. Match your ad targeting to your delivery strengths.
Improves order quality at source
07
Track Return Reasons and Fix Root Causes
Log every return reason: changed mind, unreachable, wrong product, delayed delivery, wrong address. After 50+ returns, patterns emerge. If 40% are "changed mind," your ads are overpromising or your confirmation process is weak. If 30% are "wrong address," improve your checkout form. Data beats intuition here.
Foundation for long-term improvement

Putting It Together

These tactics compound. Tactic 1 (confirmation) alone is worth implementing immediately — it's the highest ROI single action. Add tactics 2 and 3 in your first week. Track return reasons (tactic 7) from day one so you have data to act on after 30 days.

Sellers who implement all 7 consistently see delivery rates above 78%, compared to 50–60% without any optimization. At 100 orders per month, that's the difference between 55 and 78 successful deliveries — 23 extra paying customers on the same ad spend.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average COD return rate in Libya?

Without order confirmation, return rates typically sit between 25–40%. With systematic confirmation before dispatch, this drops to 15–25%, pushing delivery success above 75%.

Does order confirmation really improve delivery rates?

Yes. Phone confirmation before dispatch eliminates fake orders and impulse buyers who've already changed their minds. This single step accounts for a 15–20 percentage point improvement in delivery rates in the Libyan market.

Should I attempt redelivery on failed COD orders?

Absolutely. 30–40% of first-attempt returns succeed on a second delivery, especially when you call the customer first to confirm a new time. Don't write off returns immediately.

How quickly should I dispatch COD orders?

Aim to dispatch within 24 hours of order confirmation. COD markets are impulse-driven — every day of delay increases the chance the customer changes their mind and rejects the delivery.
