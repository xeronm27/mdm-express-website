How to Start a COD Store in Iraq From Scratch: 2026 Guide

MDM Express Team July 7, 2026 13 min read

Iraq's e-commerce market reached $1.3 billion in value in 2026 — and almost every transaction in it happens via Cash on Delivery. There are no PayPal accounts, no widespread credit cards, no Stripe checkouts. The customer pays the delivery driver at the door, or doesn't pay at all.

This isn't a barrier. It's a structure you build around. Here's exactly how.

Step 1: Choose a Product with the Right Economics

COD economics are different from regular e-commerce. You pay for shipping upfront (or per successful delivery), you run ads without guaranteed sale, and returns happen. Your product margin needs to absorb all of this.

The rule of thumb: 50% gross margin minimum. On a product sold for 30,000 IQD, at least 15,000 IQD needs to remain after product cost. From this 15,000 IQD, you pay delivery (3,000–8,000 IQD depending on city and provider), and the remainder covers your ad cost per order and profit.

Products with proven performance in Iraq COD

  • Electronics accessories: phone cases, chargers, wireless earbuds, power banks
  • Smart home devices: LED strips, mini projectors, smart plugs
  • Personal care: grooming tools, skincare devices, hair tools
  • Children's products: educational toys, activity sets
  • Kitchen and home tools: gadgets solving visible daily problems
  • Auto accessories: organizers, phone mounts, cleaning tools

Before ordering 200 units, validate with 20. Run a small ad test. See if the phone calls confirm, and if orders deliver. Scale what converts.

Step 2: Build Your Sales Page

You don't need a full e-commerce website. A single-page "sales landing page" is enough to go from zero to first 100 orders in Iraq.

Page essentials
Product images (3–5), a short punchy description of what the product does and why the customer needs it, price, and an order form. No distractions, no navigation away from the page.
Order form fields
Full name, phone number (two fields), city/governorate, specific address with landmark. The more specific the address, the higher your first-attempt delivery success.
Trust signals
A visible WhatsApp number, Arabic content, and clear "Pay on Delivery" labeling. Iraqi buyers want to know exactly what they're getting and that they can talk to someone.

Step 3: Choose Your Fulfillment Partner

This is the most important decision in your Iraq COD operation. Your fulfillment partner determines your delivery rate, your cash flow, and how fast you can scale.

What to look for:

  • All 18 Iraqi provinces. You want to run ads to all of Iraq, not restrict yourself to Baghdad only.
  • Order confirmation. The single biggest driver of delivery rate. A partner who calls customers before dispatch adds 15–25 percentage points to your delivery rate.
  • 1–3 day delivery. Speed keeps impulse buyers interested. Slow delivery kills conversion.
  • Weekly cash transfers. Never accept a monthly payout. It locks your working capital and stops you from reinvesting in stock and ads.

Step 4: Launch and Test Your Ads

Facebook and Meta remain the dominant advertising channel for Iraq COD. TikTok is growing fast, particularly for younger audiences (18–30).

Start narrow, not broad. Launch with 2 ad creatives, $15–25/day total budget, and a specific audience (interest-based, not "all Iraq"). Run for 5–7 days before making decisions. Thin budget spread across 5+ ads produces noise, not data.

Video outperforms static images in Iraq significantly. A 20–30 second video demonstrating the product solves a problem — filmed simply, with clear audio or Arabic subtitles — converts far better than any graphic.

Geographic targeting note: start with Baghdad (largest city, highest delivery infrastructure quality). Expand to Basra, Erbil, and other major cities once you have a proven product and delivery operation.

Step 5: Measure, Learn, Repeat

Two numbers define a COD operation's health:

  • Delivery rate: what percentage of shipped orders are successfully received. Target 70%+; aim for 78%+.
  • Cost per delivered order: total ad spend ÷ delivered orders. This is your true customer acquisition cost.

Review both weekly. If delivery rate drops below 65%, investigate — is it a specific city? A specific product? A confirmation process problem? If cost per delivered order goes above your margin, the math doesn't work and something needs to change in the ad targeting or product pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do I need to start a COD store in Iraq?

$300–500 for initial stock testing plus $100–200 for ads is enough to validate a product. Keep costs low until you confirm what converts. Many successful Iraq COD operations scaled from a $500 initial test.

Do I need a registered business to sell COD in Iraq?

For small-scale testing, many sellers start informally. As you scale, having a registered entity becomes important for reliable partnerships and managing cash flows from your fulfillment provider.

Which products sell best via COD in Iraq?

Electronics accessories, smart home devices, personal care tools, and kitchen gadgets perform well. Target 50%+ margins to cover delivery costs (3,000–8,000 IQD per order) and advertising costs.

Which ad platform works best for Iraq COD?

Facebook/Meta is the primary channel. TikTok is growing fast for younger demographics. Start with Facebook, then test TikTok once you have a product that demonstrably converts in the market.
