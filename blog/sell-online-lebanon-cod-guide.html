The Crisis Is Your Market Structure

The Lebanese banking crisis, which began in 2019, fundamentally reshaped how Lebanese consumers transact. Without access to online banking and with limited card usage, cash became the default for all but the most tech-savvy buyers.

Practical Setup for Lebanon COD

Pricing strategy

Price in US dollars. State the price clearly in USD on your product page and order form. Delivery agents can accept cash in USD or at the prevailing exchange rate — agree with your fulfillment partner on the conversion protocol before launch.

Product selection

Lebanese buyers are sophisticated and value-conscious — they comparison-shop. Your product needs a clear, demonstrable benefit and a price that makes sense in USD terms. Electronics accessories, personal care, home improvement tools, and imported goods with a brand story perform well.

Ad channels

Facebook and Instagram reach Lebanon's 4M+ social media users effectively. The market is small enough that audience saturation can happen quickly — rotate creatives regularly and target specific districts rather than all of Lebanon if your delivery partner has coverage gaps.

Check your delivery partner's coverage before you target an area. Lebanon's geography (mountains, remote villages, southern regions) creates delivery complexity. Make sure your fulfillment partner explicitly covers the areas you're targeting in ads.

Keys to Profitability in Lebanon

  • Order confirmation is essential. Call every customer before dispatch. Lebanon has genuine logistical complexity — confirmation filters out problematic orders before you spend on delivery.
  • Keep delivery windows tight. Lebanese buyers respond well to next-day and same-day delivery promises. A 1–2 day maximum in Beirut and major cities is the target.
  • Price competitively in USD. Buyers can easily check international prices. Offer genuine value, not a markup that local buyers will see through immediately.
  • Build a WhatsApp presence. Lebanon's commerce culture runs heavily on WhatsApp. A number that customers can message for questions and post-purchase support drives trust and repeat purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sell online in Lebanon despite the banking crisis?

Yes. COD bypasses the banking system entirely. Customers pay cash at delivery, and your fulfillment partner transfers the collected cash to you weekly — no bank account or payment gateway needed.

Should I price products in Lebanese pounds or US dollars?

Price in US dollars. Lebanese consumers are accustomed to USD pricing since the crisis, and it protects both you and the buyer from currency fluctuation. Most COD transactions in Lebanon now happen in fresh dollars or equivalent.

What delivery rate can I expect in Lebanon?

With order confirmation: 68–78% in Greater Beirut and major coastal cities. Remote mountain areas: 55–65%. Confirmation calls before dispatch are the most important single driver of delivery rate.

How important is WhatsApp for Lebanon COD sales?

Very important. Lebanese commerce culture runs heavily on WhatsApp. A visible number for customer questions before and after purchase builds significant trust — and drives higher conversion rates than markets where buyers are comfortable with anonymous checkout.
