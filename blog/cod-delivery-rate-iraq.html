Why the Numbers Vary So Widely

COD delivery rate isn't a fixed market characteristic — it's largely an operational output. Two sellers selling the same product in the same city can have a 30 percentage point difference in delivery rate, based purely on how they handle confirmation, dispatch speed, and address collection.

The sellers at 40–55% are usually: running ads to broad audiences, shipping immediately without calling anyone, and accepting vague addresses like "Karrada district." The sellers at 75–85% do the opposite.

The 4 Factors That Drive Your Rate

📞
Order Confirmation
Calling customers within 2–4 hours of ordering to confirm purchase intent, address, and delivery time. Filters out fake, impulse, and unreachable orders before dispatch costs are incurred.
Impact: +15–25% delivery rate
⚡
Delivery Speed
COD purchases are often impulse-driven. Delivering within 1–3 days catches the customer while motivation is high. Every day of delay increases cancellation risk by an estimated 5–8%.
Impact: Major for impulse products
📍
Address Accuracy
Vague addresses are a primary cause of failed first-attempt deliveries. Collecting specific building/floor/landmark information during confirmation dramatically reduces "couldn't find" returns.
Impact: -30% failed first attempts
🎯
Ad Targeting Quality
Broad, low-intent traffic generates more fake and impulse orders. Targeting specific interests and behaviors — and geographies where your delivery partner excels — improves the quality of every order.
Impact: Improves order quality at source

City-by-City Rate Expectations

Delivery rates also vary by city, driven by infrastructure quality and courier coverage depth:

  • Baghdad: Highest delivery rates — 72–82% with confirmation. Best infrastructure, most competition among couriers.
  • Basra: Strong rates — 68–78%. Major commercial center, well-served by national providers.
  • Erbil: 65–75%. Kurdish region has distinct logistics but good coverage from major providers.
  • Mosul, Kirkuk: 60–70%. Improving infrastructure, solid results with the right provider.
  • Remote provinces: 50–65%. Coverage varies; choose a provider with explicit presence in target areas.

5 Ways to Improve Your Rate This Week

01

Implement phone confirmation today. If you're not confirming every order before dispatch, add this first. It's the highest-ROI single change available to a COD seller.

02

Add a second phone number field. One unreachable customer number shouldn't kill a delivery. Give yourself a backup.

03

Dispatch within 24 hours of confirmation. Set an internal rule: confirmed orders go out the same or next day. No exceptions.

04

Log every return reason. You can't improve what you don't measure. After 30 days, patterns emerge that tell you exactly what to fix next.

05

Reattempt returns before writing them off. Call the customer, understand the situation, and redeliver. 30–40% of first-attempt returns can be converted on a second attempt.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average COD delivery rate in Iraq?

The market average sits at 60–70%. Without order confirmation, expect 40–55%. With confirmation and optimized operations, the best sellers reach 75–85%.

What is the single most effective improvement?

Order confirmation by phone before dispatch. Calling within 2–4 hours of each order to confirm intent, address, and delivery time eliminates 15–25% of orders that would otherwise fail at the door.

Why do rates vary so much between sellers?

Three main factors: order confirmation practice, delivery speed, and address accuracy. Sellers who optimize all three consistently hit 80%+, while those who optimize none hover at 50–60%.

What is a "good" COD delivery rate in Iraq?

Above 70% is respectable. Above 75% is good. Above 80% is excellent and puts you in the top tier of COD sellers operating in the Iraqi market.
